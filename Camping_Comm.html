The district camping committee provides outdoor programs that most units are unable to provide on their own, and outdoor programs are often the main reason youth join a Scout unit. From Cub Scout day camp to high-adventure programs, this committee helps make exciting outdoor programs available for Cub Scouts, Boy Scouts, and Venturers.

Tasks of the Camp Committee include:

  1. Understand each unit’s camping and outdoor record.
  2. Implement the council’s outdoor promotion plan in the district.
  3. Work with commissioners to help packs, troops, teams, and crews plan a year-round schedule of camping and outdoor program events.
  4. Promote use of camperships.
  5. Give guidance on health and safety concerns.
  6. Promote youth participation in camping and outdoor programs:
    1. Boy Scout resident camp
    2. national high-adventure programs
    3. Cub Scout outdoor program
    4. Venturing outdoor program.
  7. Assist the council committee with maintaining and developing quality outdoor program facilities.
  8. Promote units earning the National Summertime Pack Award and the National Camping Award.
  9. Supervise use of off-council campsites by permits and inspections.
  10. Track and attain camping and outdoor objectives.
  11. Implement the planned council camping and outdoor programs.
  12. Guide the Order of the Arrow, through the chapter officers and adviser, to help promote camping in the district.
