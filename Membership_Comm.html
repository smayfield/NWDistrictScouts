The district membership committee gathers information on prospective chartered organizations, helps organize new units, reorganizes dropped units and units not meeting, and recruits new members in a systematic way. It establishes and maintains mutually beneficial relationships with major community organizations and strategic alliances, both those with and without Scouting units. These include religious, educational, civic, fraternal, and veteran organizations and associations; labor unions; business and industry; professional societies; and other organizations with objectives compatible with the Boy Scouts of America.

Tasks of the Membership Committee include:
1. Establish a year-round plan for unit and membership growth.
2. Recruit and train new-unit organizers.
3. Work with district training teams to provide new units with trained personnel.
4. Plan and conduct youth and chartered organization surveys.
5. Cultivate relationships with potential chartered organizations and community groups.
6. Share with other district leaders how to work effectively with various types of organizations.
7. Organize new packs, troops, teams, and crews to meet the needs
of serving youth in your district.
8. Analyze district membership figures for all program levels.
9. Be sure a new unit is under the care of a commissioner before the organizer leaves.
10. Conduct membership events in the district:
(a) roundup plans, (b) Together plan, (c) relationships conferences.

Goals

The District Membership Committee works to ensure a steady, balanced membership growth in the District by mobilizing volunteers and developing program to recruit youth and adults into our District Scouting programs. Our membership committee goal is to support Erie Shores Council's Long Term Strategic Membership Plan to accomplish the following over the next 3 - 5 years:

  • - Maintain or Increase the Percentage of Youth Recruited and Retained in NW OH District.
    - Maintain or Increase the Number of units rechartered in NW OH District.
    - Support council programs to recruit parent volunteers.
    - Establish relationships with culturally diverse organizations and other organizations
      with the intent of raising BSA and Scouting program awareness and interest.

Promotional Items

Square Knot flier

This flyer can be used to promote Scouting. Using heavy card stock, print the flyer on two-sided paper so that when it is cut into small squares - the promotional material (with the scout logo) sits on one side and the square knot instructions sits on the other. Once printed, each card should have a hole punched in the upper left hand side and a small string should be inserted into the hole and tied into a square knot. The current flyer text is good through the end of October 29th, 2011; it promotes the Halloween Party at Miakonda. Packs and Troops can also update the document with their own contact information and use this to promote their own packs/troops or to promote scouting in general. The flyer can be distributed to bring awareness to scouting opportunities and promote scouting in our community. Questions can be directed to Donna Fisher, NW District Membership Committee chair.
